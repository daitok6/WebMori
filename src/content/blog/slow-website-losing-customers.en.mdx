## The 3-second rule

According to Google, **53% of users leave a site if it takes more than 3 seconds to load**. That means a slow website could be turning away more than half your potential customers before they even see what you offer.

In Japan, over 70% of web traffic comes from smartphones. People browsing on the train, during lunch, or before bed won't wait around for a slow site to load.

## How a slow site hurts your bottom line

### More abandoned carts

For e-commerce sites, slow product or checkout pages mean customers give up before completing their purchase. Amazon found that **a 0.1-second delay in page load costs 1% in sales**.

### Lower search rankings

Since 2021, Google uses "Core Web Vitals" — page speed and interactivity metrics — as a ranking factor. A slow site means your competitors show up above you in search results.

### Damaged brand perception

A slow site unconsciously makes visitors think "Is this company reliable?" For first-time visitors especially, your site's speed is a proxy for your professionalism.

## How slow is your site, really?

Here's a quick way to check without any tools:

**Open your site on your phone.** Count the seconds until the page fully loads. If it takes more than 3 seconds, there's room to improve.

Common causes include:

**Oversized images** — Are you uploading photos straight from your camera or phone? A single image at several megabytes can dramatically slow down your page.

**Too many plugins** — Using WordPress with 10+ plugins? Even inactive ones affect your site's speed.

**Distant servers** — If your hosting is overseas, every visit from a Japanese customer has built-in latency from the physical distance.

## Simple fixes anyone can do

### Compress your images

Before uploading photos to your site, reduce their file size. Free online compression tools make this easy. Aim for under 200KB per image.

### Remove unused plugins

If you're on WordPress, don't just deactivate plugins you're not using — delete them entirely. Fewer plugins means a faster site.

### Choose a Japan-based server

Pick hosting or a CDN with data centers in Japan. If you're on Shopify, you're already covered — they use a global CDN that includes Japan.

### Test your speed regularly

Make it a monthly habit to check your site's speed. Google's free "PageSpeed Insights" tool lets you test any URL in seconds.

## The bottom line

Your site's speed is the first impression you make on every visitor. A slow site is like a store that makes customers wait at the door — they'll leave without saying a word.
