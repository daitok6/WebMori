When a customer calls your business and no one picks up, what impression do you leave?

"They don't seem responsive." "Maybe I'll try somewhere else." They hang up — without saying a word.

A slow website leaves exactly the same impression. According to Google, **53% of users abandon a site that takes more than 3 seconds to load**. That means before a visitor even sees what you offer, more than half of them may already be gone.

In Japan, over 70% of web traffic comes from smartphones — people browsing on the train, during lunch breaks, before bed. They won't wait for a slow page.

## How a slow site hurts your bottom line

### More abandoned carts and lost inquiries

For e-commerce sites, slow product or checkout pages mean customers give up mid-purchase. Amazon found that **a 0.1-second delay in page load costs 1% in sales**. Contact forms have the same problem — if the page stalls after clicking "send," visitors leave wondering if their message even went through.

### Lower search rankings

Since 2021, Google uses "Core Web Vitals" — page speed and usability metrics — as a search ranking factor. If a competitor in your area has a faster site, they show up above you in search results.

### Damaged trust

A slow site unconsciously makes visitors think "Is this company reliable?" For first-time visitors especially, speed is a proxy for professionalism.

## How fast is your site, really?

You can check for free right now.

**Visit [pagespeed.web.dev](https://pagespeed.web.dev)** and enter your site's URL. You'll get a score from 0–100.

| Score | Status | What it means |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| 90+ | Good | No issues |
| 50–89 | Needs improvement | Room to optimize |
| Under 50 | Slow | Take action now |

Check the Mobile tab especially — that's where most of your visitors are coming from.

Common causes of slow scores:

**Oversized images** — Uploading photos straight from your camera? A single image at several megabytes can dramatically slow down your page.

**Too many plugins** — Using WordPress with 10+ plugins? Even inactive ones slow things down.

**Distant servers** — If your hosting is overseas, every visit from a Japanese customer has built-in latency.

## Simple fixes anyone can do

### Compress your images

Before uploading photos, reduce their file size. Free online compression tools make this easy. Aim for under 200KB per image.

### Remove unused plugins

If you're on WordPress, don't just deactivate plugins — delete them entirely. Fewer plugins means a faster site.

### Choose a Japan-based server

Pick hosting or a CDN with data centers in Japan. If you're on Shopify, you're already covered — they use a global CDN that includes Japan.

### Test your speed monthly

Make it a habit to check your site's speed once a month. A sudden drop in score usually means something changed.

Your site's speed is the first experience every visitor has with your business. A slow site is like a store that makes customers wait at the door — they leave without saying a word.
