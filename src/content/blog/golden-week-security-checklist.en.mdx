Golden Week — Japan's largest consecutive holiday period, running from late April into early May — is a time most business owners look forward to. A chance to step away, recharge, and not think about work for a few days.

But for unattended websites, it's a different story.

Automated attacks don't take holidays. Scanning tools run 24 hours a day, probing for sites with outdated software, weak passwords, or unpatched vulnerabilities. The problem isn't that attacks increase during Golden Week — it's that **owners aren't watching**. An alert that would normally get a same-day response can sit for 10 days. By the time the holiday ends, the damage has compounded.

This isn't a reason to cancel your plans. It's a reason to spend 10 minutes doing these five checks before you close the laptop.

## Why long holidays are higher risk for websites

Attackers operate on automated schedules. When they find a vulnerable site, they don't wait — they act immediately. The window between "intrusion" and "detected" is normally measured in hours. During a 10-day holiday, that window stretches dramatically.

The most common issues we see after holiday periods: plugin vulnerabilities that hadn't been patched, backup jobs that had silently failed, and Search Console security warnings that had been sitting unread for days.

All of these are preventable with a quick pre-holiday check.

## 5 things to do before Golden Week

### ☑ 1. Update all WordPress plugins and themes

If your site runs on WordPress, go to Dashboard → Updates and apply every pending plugin and theme update.

Outdated plugins are the single most common entry point for attackers. Every update contains patches for known issues — running old versions means those issues are sitting open on your site. Clear the update queue before you leave.

### ☑ 2. Review and strengthen your admin password

Check whether your admin login uses a password shared with any other service. If a breach happens anywhere else — an email provider, a shopping site, any other platform — attackers will try that credential combination against WordPress and other admin panels automatically.

Your CMS admin password should be unique and strong. If it isn't, now is the time to change it.

### ☑ 3. Verify your most recent backup

Don't assume backups are running just because you set them up. Backup jobs can silently fail — full storage, plugin updates that reset configuration, expired credentials. Log into your backup plugin or hosting panel and confirm that a recent backup actually completed successfully.

If you can't find a recent backup, create one manually before the holiday.

### ☑ 4. Check Google Search Console for security warnings

Google monitors indexed sites for malware and signs of compromise. If it finds something, it reports it in Search Console — but only if you look.

Log into [Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console), navigate to the Security Issues tab, and check for any active warnings. If something is flagged, address it before the holiday rather than letting it sit for 10 days.

### ☑ 5. Check your contact form spam filter

A sudden spike in form spam isn't just annoying — it can signal that your form has a security weakness being actively probed. Confirm that reCAPTCHA is enabled and that your form plugin is up to date.

## If you find something during the holiday

If you notice something wrong with your site while you're away, the first step is to consider **putting the site in maintenance mode**. Temporarily taking it offline limits further exposure while you arrange help. It's a better outcome than leaving a compromised site running for the rest of the holiday.

Don't try to investigate or fix a potential breach yourself without guidance — well-intentioned attempts can overwrite logs or worsen the situation. Contact a professional, describe what you've noticed, and let them lead the response.

## The short version

Golden Week should be restful. Running these five checks before you leave takes about 10 minutes and eliminates the most common causes of post-holiday headaches. Outdated plugins, reused passwords, missing backups, unchecked warnings, weak forms — each one is a simple fix now or a potentially serious problem in 10 days.

Do the check. Enjoy the holiday.
