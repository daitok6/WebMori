"We're a small business — hackers wouldn't bother with us."

The businesses that think this are actually the most vulnerable. Attackers don't manually pick targets — automated tools scan the internet for sites with weak defenses. Small businesses that haven't updated their software or secured their admin panels are exactly what those tools find.

Cars get safety inspections. People get annual checkups. But very few business owners ever give their website a regular checkup — even though it's often the first touchpoint for customers.

Check whether any of the following 5 signs apply to your site.

## Sign 1: Nothing's been updated in 6+ months

WordPress plugins, themes, Shopify apps — when did you last hit "update"?

Software updates regularly include security patches. If you haven't updated in over 6 months, **known vulnerabilities may be sitting wide open** on your site.

Think of it like leaving your front door with a broken lock for half a year.

## Sign 2: Your password is "the usual one"

Is your admin password the same one you use for other services?

If any of those services gets breached, attackers will try that same email-password combination everywhere else. This is called a "credential stuffing" attack — and it's automated.

**Your admin password should be unique — not shared with anything else.**

## Sign 3: Browsers show "Not Secure" warnings

Does your browser show a padlock with a warning, or "Not Secure" in the address bar?

This means your SSL certificate (HTTPS) isn't properly set up. To visitors, it looks like a warning that your site might be dangerous — bounce rates climb significantly. It also hurts your Google rankings.

## Sign 4: Unknown pages or links appearing on your site

Have you noticed pages or links that you didn't create?

When a site gets compromised, attackers often inject spam pages or phishing links in places that are easy to miss. You can check by searching "site:yourdomain.com" on Google to see every indexed page.

If anything looks unfamiliar, you need to act immediately.

## Sign 5: Your contact form is flooded with spam

Are you getting large volumes of junk submissions through your forms every day?

While it seems like a nuisance, mass form spam is **a sign that your form's security is weak**. Attackers can exploit vulnerable forms as a foothold for more serious attacks.

Adding reCAPTCHA or reviewing your form configuration can help significantly.
